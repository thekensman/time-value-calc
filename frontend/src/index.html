Why This Calculator Exists

Most people have never calculated what an hour of their life is actually worth. Not their salary divided by 2,080 — their real take-home pay, minus every hidden cost of working, divided by every hour their job consumes. Commute time. Getting-ready time. Decompression time. Work lunches. Professional clothes. Once you know this number, every spending decision changes. A $40 dinner isn't $40 — it's X hours of your life energy.

Methodology inspired by Your Money or Your Life by Vicki Robin & Joe Dominguez. Built by Kenneth Cross — writer, developer, and parent in the Chicago suburbs who calculated his own real hourly wage and immediately regretted how much time he'd spent at Chuck E. Cheese. Everything runs in your browser. Nothing is sent anywhere. No signup required. Tax estimates are approximations — consult a CPA for your specific situation. 🔒 Your financial data never leaves your device.

This real hourly wage calculator accounts for federal and state income taxes, FICA (Social Security and Medicare), daily commute costs and time, work clothing, work meals, childcare, decompression time, and unpaid overtime. The "Is It Worth My Time?" decision calculator helps you decide when to hire a professional versus doing it yourself — factoring in not just cost but how much you enjoy or dread the task. The job comparison tool reveals when a lower-salary remote position actually pays more per hour of life energy than a higher-salary office job.
